# Question Bank

A starting list of common interview questions, grouped by category, with the signal each one tests, the shape of a strong answer, why weak answers fail, and a derivation prompt for building the candidate's specific answer from their materials.

This is a menu, not a script. Select 6–10 questions that fit the role, round, and JD. Always include the opener. Prioritize any question confirmed from research for this specific company (tag it `Confirmed [Company] Q`). Derive every answer from the candidate's real record — see `references/voice-and-tone.md`.

How to use a derivation prompt: read it, search the candidate's materials for the named ingredients, and write the STAR or talking-points answer. If an ingredient is missing, either pick a different question or write the gap as a bracketed prompt for the candidate to fill.

---

## Openers

### "Tell me about yourself." / "Walk me through your background."
- **Signal:** Can they self-edit to what's relevant? Do they have a through-line?
- **Strong answer:** A 60-second arc with one through-line, ending at why this role now. Three phases max, each one line, then land on the present.
- **Weak answer:** A chronological resume readthrough. No theme. Runs three minutes.
- **Derive:** Find the candidate's career phases; identify the single through-line connecting them (e.g. "I build the system rather than wait for one"); end on the bridge to this role.

### "Why this company? Why this role?"
- **Signal:** Genuine, specific interest vs. spray-and-pray applicant.
- **Strong answer:** A concrete, non-obvious connection — a product they've actually used, a transformation that matches their experience, a value that fits their record — then why the role's actual mandate fits what they've done. ~60 seconds.
- **Weak answer:** Praise that could apply to any employer ("you're an industry leader," "great culture").
- **Derive:** Look for real touchpoints between candidate and company (used the product? built something adjacent? operated the same way?). Lead with the strongest one. Pull the role's actual mandate from the JD.

## Behavioral — leadership

### "Tell me about your leadership experience." / "developing other leaders."
- **Signal:** Scope, and whether they've managed *managers* vs. only ICs.
- **Strong answer:** Phased by role with concrete span (how many managers, how many ICs indirectly), plus a philosophy of how managing managers differs (you build the coaching system, you don't do the coaching).
- **Weak answer:** "I'm a servant leader who empowers my team" with no span or system.
- **Derive:** Pull exact span of control at each level; find the system they built (a program, a cadence, a framework) and the outcome it produced.

### "A time you coached someone toward independence."
- **Signal:** Do they build capability or create dependence?
- **Strong answer:** STAR. Action shows a deliberate structure (shadow → co-run → solo → step back) and the discipline to *not* rescue. Result: the person operates without them, ideally promoted.
- **Weak answer:** "I gave them feedback and they improved." No mechanism.
- **Derive:** Find a named report who grew; reconstruct the specific intervention; attribute the outcome (and any recommendation that backs it).

### "Managing through change / a reorg / leadership churn."
- **Signal:** Stability under ambiguity; protecting the team while delivering.
- **Strong answer:** STAR. Names the instability, the structural fix, and the retained performance.
- **Derive:** Find a transition the candidate stabilized; the before/after metric.

## Behavioral — execution

### "A time you navigated ambiguity / built something from zero."
- **Signal:** Bias to action without full information; first-principles thinking.
- **Strong answer:** STAR. Situation establishes "no playbook, hard deadline." Action shows working from first principles and deciding daily without complete data. Result: shipped, with the stakes protected.
- **Weak answer:** A situation that wasn't actually ambiguous, or an Action that's just "I made a plan."
- **Derive:** Find the candidate's clearest zero-to-one or crisis build; the constraint (deadline, dollars at stake); the launch outcome.

### "A time you missed a goal / a failure."
- **Signal:** Accountability and learning, not blame-shifting.
- **Strong answer:** STAR. Owns the miss plainly, shows the specific change made afterward, and the better result next time.
- **Weak answer:** A humble-brag ("I work too hard"), or blaming others.
- **Derive:** Find a real setback and the concrete process change it produced.

### "A time you delivered under pressure / fixed operational performance."
- **Signal:** Can they diagnose and fix a system under load?
- **Strong answer:** STAR. Action shows they pulled data, found the structural cause, and fixed it without brute force (e.g. cross-training vs. adding headcount). Result quantified.
- **Derive:** Find an ops turnaround; the diagnostic step; the structural fix; the metrics (resolution time, reopen rate, CSAT, no added headcount).

## Domain-specific (sample by role family)

Use the family matching the JD; derive from the candidate's domain record.

- **Support / CX:** queue health and SLA management; what the goal of a reply should be (resolution that builds trust, not just ticket closure; the reply as a product surface); deflection vs. quality; tying support to ARR/retention; VoC and cross-functional influence.
- **Engineering / technical:** a hard technical tradeoff; debugging a production incident; balancing velocity and quality; mentoring engineers; a system you designed and why.
- **Product:** a prioritization call you made and the data behind it; killing a feature; a discovery insight that changed direction; aligning eng/design/business.
- **Sales / GTM:** a complex deal you closed; a forecast you had to defend; handling a churn risk; building a territory or pipeline from scratch.
- **Operations / program:** a process you designed and scaled; a cross-functional rollout; a metric you instrumented from zero.

For each, the strong-answer shape is the same: specific situation, a system or decision (not just effort), a quantified result. Always tie back to the JD's actual priorities.

## Scenario questions

### "How would you handle [the queue at 150% capacity / a P0 incident / a team conflict]?"
- **Signal:** Real-time judgment and prioritization; do they have a framework?
- **Strong answer:** A brief framework (triage by impact, communicate, structural fix), grounded in a time they actually did it. Not purely hypothetical.
- **Weak answer:** A generic textbook process with no evidence they've lived it.
- **Derive:** Map the scenario to the closest thing the candidate has actually done; answer the hypothetical, then anchor it with "I did exactly this when…".

## Closing

### "What questions do you have for us?"
- **Signal:** Engagement and calibration to the room.
- **Strong answer:** 2–3 questions matched to who's asking (see §05 in the brief). At least one that signals strategic thinking.
- **Weak answer:** "No, you covered everything," or questions answered by the JD.
- **Derive:** Build the §05 ask groups; tell the candidate how many to actually ask.

## Reverse-screening / logistics

### "Why are you leaving / why are you looking?"
- **Signal:** Forward-looking and non-bitter.
- **Strong answer:** Brief, framed toward what they're moving *to*, not running from. No badmouthing.
- **Derive:** Frame around the pull of this role's mandate.

### "What are your comp expectations?"
- **Signal:** Preparedness and reasonableness.
- **Strong answer:** A researched number or range tied to the role's band, delivered calmly. Defer specifics to the recruiter process when appropriate.
- **Derive:** Use the comp range from research; set a target; add a watch-point note to "know your number before the call."

### "Where the role is a manager-of-managers stretch / an apparent gap."
- **Signal:** Honesty and how they close distance.
- **Strong answer:** Lead with the strongest adjacent proof, not a defensive bridge. If it's a real gap, name it and show the nearest experience and how fast they'd close it.
- **Derive:** Decide gap vs. strength from the record; if strength, lead with span and system; if gap, be honest (see honesty rules in `voice-and-tone.md`).
